# Focus Management

## Question 1: What is Deep Work?

Deep work is when one focus completely at one task without any distractions.
It also means avoiding shallow task like checking mails, scrolling phone etc.
Deep work saves a lot of time too.

Cal Newport says this type of focus is rare today, it makes you valuable in your career.

---


## Question 2: How to do deep work properly?

### 1. Take breaks
Avoid trying to concentrate all day. After **60–90 minutes** of intense work, take a serious break.

### 2. Avoiding interruptions
Move your phone to a different room.  Instruct others not to bother you.

### 3. Establish a schedule
Do deep work every day at the same time. Your brain adjusts to it.

### 4. Put an end to checking phone
When working deeply, avoid checking your email or messages. You become distracted each time you switch.

## Question 3: How can I use this in my daily life?

Schedule deep work time daily (10 AM – 12 AM). Avoid distraction at this time.

Storing phone in my bag or putting it on Airplane Mode. 

Starting slow, If 1 hour seems excessive, begin with 25 minutes and work my way up.

Planning break like:
- Walk  
- checking important mails

## Question 4: What are the dangers of social media?

### 1. Kills focus
 Social media apps grab attention. Using them makes it harder to focus on work.

### 2. Makes you compare
Seeing everyone's happy moments makes you feel your life is boring.

### 3. Wastes time
You open phone for **2 minutes**, end up scrolling **40 minutes**.

### 4. Reduces attention span
These days short form content reduces the attention span.
