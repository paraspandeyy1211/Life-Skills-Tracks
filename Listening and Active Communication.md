# Listening and Active Communication

---



## 1. Active Listening

### Question 1  
### What are the steps or strategies to practice Active Listening?

 1. Try not to be distracted by your own thoughts. Instead, Focus on the speaker and what they are saying.

 2. Do not try to interrupt the other person. Let them finish their statement and then respond accordingly.

 3. Use phrases that show that you are interested which helps the other person continue speaking.

 4. Show that you are actively listening to other person with your body language, such as maintaining eye contact etc.

 5. If necessary take notes during important conversations.

 6. Always paraphrase what other has said to make sure you are on the same page.

## 2. Reflective Listening

### Question 2  
### According to Fisher's model, what are the key points of Reflective Listening?

1. Focus on the speaker what they are talking and not to think about the reply while the talk.

2. Pay attention not only to what is being said but also to how it is being said, on Speaker's body language etc.

3. Paraphrase what the speaker said in your own words to confirm that you understood it correctly.

4. Asking the speaker if your understanding is same as what speaker was trying to convey and clarify it immediately.

5. Respond calmly after the speaker has finish talking.

6. Give a response that shows you have understood the message and the emotion behind it.ind it.

## 3. Reflection

### Question 3  
### What are the obstacles in your listening process?

1. Sometimes I think about what I want to say next instead of fully listening.

2. External distractions like mobile phone, some outside noises make not listen properly.

3. In technical discussions, overexposure to information at once sometimes lead to missing important points.

4. I may assume I already understand the topic and stop listening carefully.


---

### Question 4  
### What can you do to improve your listening?


1. Stop all distractions and focus only on the conversation.

2. Practice paraphrasing to confirm understanding.

3. Let the speaker finish before responding.

4. Asking clarifying questions instead of guessing.
